# Claude Code Champion Kit

A playbook for individual engineers already using Claude Code who want to help their team adopt it. Covers what to share, how to answer questions, and how to grow adoption sustainably.

## Key Points

- The champion role has three reinforcing behaviors: share discoveries publicly, answer questions with concrete prompts, and grow the circle through lightweight recurring habits ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- Time commitment is approximately 40 minutes per week: 15 minutes posting wins, 20 minutes answering questions, 5 minutes for a weekly thread, and 0–30 minutes for optional pairing ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- The most useful posts describe reusable techniques a colleague can apply tomorrow, not completed outcomes; techniques compound as they spread, status updates do not ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- When colleagues ask how something was done, the most useful response is the actual prompt used rather than an explanation ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- Point people at the feature (e.g., "press Shift+Tab") rather than the documentation; they will find deeper resources on their own once unblocked ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- Adoption that depends on a single person is fragile; adoption carried by shared habits compounds on its own ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- For security and data-handling questions, refer colleagues to the organization's administrator rather than improvising an answer ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]

## Quotes

- "The work you do as a champion has a disproportionate effect: every example you share shortens the learning curve for the engineers who come after you, and every question you answer in public turns one person's experience into something the whole team can build on." ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- "Documentation tells people what is possible; your posts show them what is actually working in your environment." ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- "A concrete, runnable example removes the gap between curiosity and a first successful use, which is where most adoption efforts stall." ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]
- "The colleague who asks you the most questions is usually ready to take on this role." ^[raw/document/claude code/claude-code-037-champion-kit-2026-04-29.md]

## Notes

- The source includes a thirty-day playbook (seed channel, start rhythm, pair and consolidate, hand off) and a quick-reference table of techniques for moving users from first trial to daily use.
- The quick-reference sheet covers: providing context via `@file`, using plan mode, running `/init`, reusing skills via `SKILL.md`, configuring Stop hooks, pasting error output rather than rephrasing, and keeping edits surgical.
