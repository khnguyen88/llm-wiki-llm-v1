# Model Distillation

Distillation is a technique for creating smaller, more efficient models from larger ones by training a "student" model to replicate the outputs of a "teacher" model. It has gained significant traction, most notably with DeepSeek.

## How It Works

1. A large, capable "teacher" model generates outputs (responses, reasoning traces, etc.).
2. A smaller "student" model is trained on these outputs rather than raw data.
3. The result is a model that is smaller and faster than the teacher while maintaining similar accuracy and response quality.

## Naming Convention

Models that have been distilled typically include `-distilled`, `-Distill`, or similar in their names:
- `deepseek-r1-distill-llama-70b` → Distilled using Llama 70B as the teacher.
- `DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B` → A Qwen 2.5 32B model fine-tuned on 800,000 chain-of-thought reasoning samples generated by DeepSeek-R1 (671B).

## Famous Example

`DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B` outperforms OpenAI o1-mini on multiple benchmarks despite being ~20x smaller.

## Key Insight

When you see "-Distill" in a name, it means the model learned its skills from a bigger model, not just from raw data. The "distillation economy" is a major trend in 2026: DeepSeek-R1 proved you can get 80%+ of frontier reasoning in a 7-32B model, and now everyone is distilling.
