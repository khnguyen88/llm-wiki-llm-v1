# BYOAI

Bring Your Own AI — the principle that users should be able to plug any AI model into their personal knowledge base, rather than being locked into a single provider's system. This includes using Claude, Codex, OpenCode, or even finetuning an open source model on the wiki data. ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:9]

## Key Points

- Users can choose any AI to process their information — Claude, Codex, OpenCode, or others ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:9]
- An open source AI can be finetuned on the wiki, so in principle the model could "know" the user in its weights rather than merely attending over the data ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:9]
- BYOAI creates competitive pressure on AI providers — users can switch models freely ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:11]
- This principle depends on the other LLM Wiki pillars: data must be in universal formats (File over App) and under user control (Yours) for BYOAI to be possible ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:7-9]

## Details

BYOAI is the fourth pillar of the LLM Wiki personalization pattern. It is enabled by the preceding pillars: data must be explicit and navigable (Explicit), under the user's control (Yours), and in universal formats (File over App) before it becomes meaningful to plug in any AI. The strongest form of BYOAI is finetuning an open source model on the wiki data, which would embed user knowledge directly in the model's weights rather than relying on context-window retrieval. ^[raw/articles/farzapedia.md:4-9]
