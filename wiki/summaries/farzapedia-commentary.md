# Farzapedia Commentary

## Key Points

- Karpathy's commentary on Farza's Farzapedia (a personal wikipedia) as an example of the LLM Wiki pattern in action
- Four advantages over "status quo" AI personalization (where the AI allegedly gets better the more you use it):

1. **Explicit**: The memory artifact (the wiki) is explicit and navigable — you can inspect exactly what the AI knows and doesn't know
2. **Yours**: Your data stays on your local computer, not locked in an AI provider's system. You control your information
3. **File over app**: Simple files in universal formats (markdown, images) — interoperable with any tool, CLI, or viewer
4. **BYOAI**: Use any AI to plug into this information — Claude, Codex, OpenCode, etc. Could even finetune an open-source model on your wiki

- Acknowledges this is not the simplest approach (requires managing file directories) but argues that "agent proficiency" is a core 21st-century skill
- Expects products to emerge that make the workflow easier, but the file-over-app foundation should remain

## Quote

> "This approach to personalization puts _you_ in full control. The data is yours. In Universal formats. Explicit and inspectable. Use whatever AI you want over it, keep the AI companies on their toes!"
